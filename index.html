Local journalism in Ukraine: The eyes of the war and the voice and soul of the country.


A journalist stands atop destroyed vehicle after fights between Russian and Ukrainian forces in Bucha,outskirts of Kyiv, Ukraine, April 6, 2022. (Photo by Felipe Dana/Associated Press)


BUCHA, Ukraine — More than 250 local journalists from cities and towns across Ukraine as well as international human rights advocates and government officials gathered here today along with scores of international journalists for the second annual Bucha Journalism Conference titled


The attendees gathered in an amphitheater in the central park in city of Bucha, which became a war crimes by Russian forces in the earliest days of the full-scale invasion in 2022. Now, Bucha stand as living representation of the fierce determination of Ukraine to rebuild from the trail of destruction and death that Russian troops left here. There is a palpable defiance & resilience in third year of fighting since 2022 invasion in war that as everyone here will quickly tell you truly 10 years ago with Russian & occupation of Crimea. In opening conference Sevgil Musaeiva, editor-chief of Ukrainska Pravda, which is GroundTruth partner newsroom through our program Report for the World, said, “We are the eyes of the war & voice & some ways the soul of the country.”


Sevgil Musaeiva, the editor-in-chief of Ukrainska Pravda delivers her remarks during the 2024 Bucha Journalism Conference. (Photo by Charles Sennott/GroundTruth)


Evidence that the war is raging on was made loud and clear today when an air-raid siren sounded as the journalists gathered here were receiving text alerts of Russian attacks in the city of Kharkiv in the northeast and as the whole country was holding its breath while waiting for the newest round of military aid to arrive to defend against a rising fear that Russia is trying to seize this moment to brace for more concentrated attacks. The all-clear came soon after the sirens, and the conference continued on, but the reports from the front kept coming that a new day of intense fighting was unfolding.


Safety for journalists in the field was a primary concern here as the war grinds on. To date, 17 journalists have been killed covering the war since early 2022. There is a need for more protective equipment, such as Kevlar vests and helmets, and first-aid training and more first-aid kits.


But there was no fatigue here as journalists shared intensity & urgency documenting war crimes & fending off assaults on journalists in field & often on truth itself.


The journalists here discussed difficult balancing act they face simultaneously covering war against the country while also paying attention to ongoing allegations of corruption by the Ukrainian government & its response, which too often attempts to try discredit & threaten journalists who are revealing misuse of funds by the government & particularly by scandal-plagued military leadership.


But of all the themes addressed at Bucha conference, one that stood out was importance of local news organizations in this war as ones who serve communities on front lines and closest to stories that reveal the layers of corruption.“Fear among local journalists is greater, I believe, that the national journalists based in Kyiv. “We live closer to frontline & we feel the pressure from law enforcement officials who want to restrict our ability to report.”
